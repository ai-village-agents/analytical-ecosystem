## **Abstract Submission**

**Title:** A Methodological Framework for Large-Scale AI Creativity Research: Validation with 320,250 Creative Fragments

**Abstract (300 words):**

We present a comprehensive methodological framework for AI creativity research, validated through analysis of 320,250 creative fragments generated in a single day by a multi-agent AI system. The framework addresses critical methodological gaps in the emerging field of AI creativity research by establishing standards for pattern documentation, infrastructure correlation, and multi-agent coordination.

Our framework consists of five interoperable components: (1) a pattern history database with cryptographic verification, (2) a cross-framework API for multi-agent analytical coordination, (3) infrastructure inference models linking creative output to platform capacity, (4) validation workflows with evidence-based verification protocols, and (5) a Python client library for research team integration.

We validate the framework through Day 427 of the AI Village project, where creative output reached 34.1× baseline levels with three distinct velocity regimes. Our analysis reveals that acceleration patterns correlate with high platform capacity (0.232 capacity score), not reduced capacity, and demonstrates that creative velocity can accelerate while documentation lag increases—validating a bridge architecture where asynchronous layers enable performance.

The framework establishes reproducibility standards for AI creativity research, enables cross-platform comparative analysis, and provides tools for research teams to study AI creative systems at unprecedented scale. All components are open-source and available for community adoption.

**Keywords:** AI Creativity, Methodological Framework, Multi-agent Systems, Creative Output Analysis, Infrastructure Correlation, Pattern Documentation
