# Community Launch Templates

Use these ready-to-send templates to announce the framework to different communities. Replace placeholders before sending. Each includes a call-out to the Day 427 validation context to signal recency and rigor.

---

## 1) Academic Announcement (NeurIPS / ICML / ACL audiences)
- **Channel:** Department/venue mailing lists, lab forums, targeted faculty emails.
- **Subject:** `[Announcement] {Framework Name} open for collaboration — validated on Day 427 milestone`
- **Body:**
  ```
  Hi {Name/Colleagues},

  We are releasing {Framework Name}, designed for {core capability}. The system completed Day 427 validation focusing on {validation focus} with results at {link to report/paper}.

  Highlights:
  - Benchmarks: {key metrics or leaderboard entries}
  - Reproducibility: {repo link} | {dataset link} | {docker/env link}
  - Alignment with {venue topical area}: {short note}

  Invitation:
  - Looking for collaborators on {research directions}
  - Demo session: {date/time}, {location or virtual link}

  Materials: {paper/preprint link}, {poster/slides link}, {code}.

  Thanks for considering this for {NeurIPS/ICML/ACL} audiences.
  {Your Name}, {Affiliation}
  ```

## 6) Blog Post Template (framework introduction)
- **Title:** `{Framework Name}: a {short descriptor} — validated on Day 427`
- **Outline:**
  1. Problem and motivation
  2. What’s new in {Framework Name}
  3. Day 427 validation summary (methods, metrics, key results)
  4. Architecture and design choices
  5. Quick start (code snippets, install, sample run)
  6. Benchmarks and comparisons
  7. Roadmap and how to contribute
  8. Calls to action: try the demo, file issues, join the community
- **CTA block:** `Try it now: {repo/docs link} | Validation report: {link}`

---

## 7) Conference Presentation Template (slides + speaking notes)
- **Slide flow:**
  1. Title: `{Framework Name} — Day 427 validated`
  2. Problem statement and audience
  3. Core approach and architecture
  4. Day 427 validation: setup, datasets, metrics, outcomes
  5. Demos (screens/video or live)
  6. Comparisons and ablations
  7. Roadmap and requests for collaboration
  8. Links/QR: repo, paper, validation report, contact
- **Speaker notes highlights:**
  - Emphasize Day 427 validation as evidence of readiness.
  - Call out reproducibility assets (code, data, checkpoints).
  - Invite questions on evaluation methods and deployment.
